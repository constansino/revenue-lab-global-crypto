Referral Message Rule

Make the buyer's job to copy, send, and connect, not to retell the whole project.

A good warm-intro referral message names the fit, uses one public-safe proof link, keeps the hidden boundary explicit, and ends with one clear motion in under a minute.

Best Formats

These are the five client-forward message shapes that usually move fastest.

Warm DM

Best when the buyer has a direct chat relationship and wants the lightest possible introduction.

Shared Thread

Best when the buyer can connect both sides in one thread and make the handoff visible immediately.

Email Intro

Best when the referred buyer expects more context and usually works in longer-form written communication.

Team Context Note

Best when the buyer wants to float the idea inside a team before making a direct one-to-one introduction.

Async Bump

Best when the intro already went out but the new buyer did not move and one polite nudge is needed.
