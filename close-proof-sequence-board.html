Proof-to-payment sequence

Send the shortest proof that clears doubt, then move to payment.

Prospects stall when too much proof arrives in no order. This page fixes the sequence: identify the kind of doubt, send one matching proof asset, confirm fit, then move to the payment path without reopening the whole conversation.

Sequence Rule

Proof should clear doubt, not create another research project.

The best close sequence is simple. Match the doubt, send one proof asset, and once the buyer is clear, stop feeding more proof and ask for the next decision.

Bad Sequences

These proof habits slow the close instead of helping it.

Too Many Links

Dumping the whole library creates homework. Send the one asset that matches the current doubt.

Proof With No Ask

Proof should lead to a next decision. If the proof lands, ask for the move instead of waiting for vague momentum.

Payment Too Early

Do not send payment while the buyer is still unconvinced. Clear the main doubt first, then switch modes.
