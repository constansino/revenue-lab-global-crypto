Intake Rule

Referral intake should inherit the paid step, not restart discovery through the original buyer.

The buyer already approved a narrow first step. Good intake keeps that step explicit, asks only for the inputs that shape delivery, and preserves who owns questions and whether the referrer stays visible once work starts.

Minimum Inputs

These are usually enough to start a paid referred first step cleanly.

Tx Hash

Keep the confirmed transfer attached to intake so the startup packet always points back to a real paid event.

Approved First Step

Name the exact first step that was bought so intake does not quietly mutate into a wider discovery project.

Main Bottleneck

Ask for the single visible problem that should improve first once work starts.

Desired Outcome

Describe the concrete shift the buyer wants from this first step, not a generic long-term vision statement.

Relevant Links

Capture only the assets that help execute this paid step now: live pages, docs, screenshots, repos, or recordings.

Decision Owner And Boundary

Keep one person accountable for quick answers and make it clear whether replies stay direct, shared, or visible to the referrer.

Do Not Do This

Most referral intake drag comes from collecting the wrong kind of detail.

Do Not Restart Discovery

The buyer already bought a narrow first step. Intake should not become a fresh strategy workshop.

Do Not Ask For Every Asset

Pull only the material needed for this step or the intake packet becomes another sorting problem.

Do Not Lose The Paid Scope

Keep the approved first step visible inside intake so kickoff does not expand into adjacent work by accident.

Do Not Blur Owner And Referrer Boundary

If nobody knows who answers or whether the original buyer stays visible, intake will not save the start from drift.
