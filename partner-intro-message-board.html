Best Formats

These are the five message shapes that usually move fastest.

Warm DM

Best when the partner already has a direct chat relationship and wants the lightest possible intro.

Shared Thread

Best when the partner can add both sides to one message and keep the transition visible.

Email Intro

Best when the buyer expects more context and the partner usually works in longer-form written communication.

Group Context

Best when the partner introduces the idea inside a team or group chat before a direct connection happens.

Async Bump

Best when the first intro landed but nobody moved, and the partner needs one polite follow-up line.

What To Include

Most good partner intro messages fit inside these boundaries.

Exact Buyer Pattern

Name the specific bottleneck or buyer type, not a broad market category.

One Proof Line

Use one approved result summary, not a long case-study dump.

One Main Link

Send the strongest single page instead of a stack of tabs.

One Owner

State who replies next so the intro does not hang in ambiguity.

One Next Step

Review, reply, connect, or pay. Pick one motion and write it clearly.

No Hidden Details

Avoid unapproved brand names, internal economics, or white-label details the partner never cleared for reuse.
