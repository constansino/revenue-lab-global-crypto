Before one closed warm-intro win turns into a public story, freeze the boundary that keeps the lane safe.

A warm-intro result can be commercially useful on a public page, but that does not mean the whole private story should go public. This page helps choose the smallest safe public version: a trust proof line, a brandless public proof card, a quote-led mini story, or a fuller case-study route only when the lane is truly safe.

Public Story Rule

The best public warm-intro proof is usually smaller than the private story behind it.

A public story should answer five things cleanly: why it needs to be public, what can be shown, what stays hidden, where it will live, and what should still stay inside repeat lanes or private proof.

Public-Safe Formats

Most warm-intro wins should widen into one of these smaller public forms first.

Trust Proof Line

One short public line that makes the process feel credible without unpacking the full private win.

Brandless Public Card

A generic before-after card that shows the shift without naming the buyer or exposing the delivery context.

Quote-Led Mini Story

One approved named or role-based line plus a small amount of public-safe context.

Anonymous Public Story

A narrow public page that names the account type and the shift while keeping the buyer fully hidden.

Case-Study Escalation

A fuller public story only when the trust boundary and naming boundary are clearly settled.

Stay Private Instead

If public use adds more exposure than leverage, keep the proof in repeat lanes, reply threads, or the library instead.
