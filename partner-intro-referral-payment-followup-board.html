Follow-Up Rule

A referred deal can still cool after the yes if the transfer path stays vague.

Good referral payment follow-up removes the last operational friction. Confirm who sends funds, what exactly the deposit covers, which network and wallet route should be used, and what happens the moment the hash is shared back.

Common Referral Leaks

Most pending transfers in a referred lane stall for one of these reasons.

No Named Payer

The referred team may be aligned, but nobody named the exact person who is supposed to open the wallet and send.

Approval And Payment Are Split

One person said yes while another person controls the funds, and the handoff between them was never made operationally clear.

Wallet Friction Stayed Unspoken

The payer may be ready in principle but paused on network, wallet, exchange, or address confidence.

The Referrer Gets Dragged Back In

The original buyer should not have to chase transfer timing between the new deal owner and the payer.

No Exact Send Window

Referred deals drift when the follow-up asks for updates instead of asking when the transfer will actually move.

Short Follow-Up Lines

Use direct lines that move the transfer without reopening the whole referred deal.

Payer Line

Since the first step is already approved, the only thing I want to confirm is who is actually sending the transfer.

Route Line

If the remaining blocker is the wallet route, I can keep it to one clean path: network, address, transfer, tx hash.

Scope Line

The deposit is tied to the exact first paid step already agreed, not to a wider undefined engagement.

Boundary Line

The original referrer does not need to stay in the middle here unless they specifically want to help re-anchor trust.

Timing Line

If you still want the current window, the useful next move is the transfer and hash rather than another abstract check-in.

Proof Line

Once funds move, send the tx hash in the same thread and I will switch this directly into proof and activation.

Do Not Do This

Most transfer delays get worse because the follow-up is too broad, too soft, or routed through the wrong person.

Do Not Reopen The Whole Referral Story

If the referred buyer is already aligned, do not go back to the full pitch just because the transfer is late.

Do Not Ask The Referrer To Chase Payment

The original buyer should not become the default collection layer between the active deal owner and the payer.

Do Not Ask For Vague Updates

Ask for the exact blocker or the exact send timing instead of asking for generic thoughts.

Do Not Send Multiple Wallet Routes

One clean payment path is easier to execute than several options dropped into a warm but low-attention thread.

Do Not Start On A Promise Alone

Keep the start tied to the transfer proof so the referred deal stays commercially clean.
