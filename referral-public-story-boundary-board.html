Referral Public Story Rule

The best public referral proof is usually smaller than the private relationship path behind it.

A public story should answer five things cleanly: why it needs to be public, what can be shown, what stays hidden, where it will live, and what should still remain inside trust or proof routes.

Public-Safe Formats

Most referral-safe wins should widen into one of these smaller public forms first.

Trust Proof Line

One short public line that makes the process feel credible without unpacking the full private win.

Bounded Proof Card

A compact before-after block that shows movement without exposing the buyer or delivery context.

Quote-Led Mini Story

One approved role-based line plus a small amount of public-safe context.

Narrow Public Story

A small public page that names the account type and the shift while keeping the buyer and referral path hidden.

Case Escalation

A fuller public story only when the trust and naming boundary are clearly settled.

Stay Smaller Instead

If public use adds more exposure than leverage, keep the proof in trust or proof routes instead.
