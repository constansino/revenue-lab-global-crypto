The Problem

Most teams do not lose money on documents because PDFs are hard. They lose money because the process is sloppy.

Scattered intake

Files arrive in too many places, names are inconsistent, and operators waste time just figuring out what is missing.

No clean redaction step

Teams often share raw documents when they only needed a safe, redacted copy for review, proof, or partner handoff.

Approval is invisible

Nobody knows whether a file is waiting on the client, waiting on internal approval, ready to sign, or safe to deliver.

Workflow

The narrow wedge: intake, redact, approve, deliver.

1. Secure intake link

Give the client one place to upload. Ask for the exact file types and fields you need instead of letting them guess.

2. Auto-sort the submission

Group documents into predictable buckets like identity, contract, invoice, compliance, and delivery attachments.

3. Create redacted copies

Generate a safe version for internal review, external sharing, or proof capture without exposing the raw file.

4. Move through approval states

Track waiting, approved, revision needed, ready to sign, and delivered without chasing status in chat.

5. Ship a clean delivery package

Send the final link, timestamp, and next action clearly so the client knows what was delivered and what happens next.
