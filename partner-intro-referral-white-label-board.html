Client-referral white-label page

Protect the referred lane when the work is real but the buyer, referrer, and operating mechanics should not become visible.

Some client-referred deals are commercially strong and still sensitive. The proof is useful, the buyer path is real, and the referrer does not need to become part of delivery or public proof. This page locks that down: define who is visible, what remains private, which proof layer is safe, and how close-out materials can travel without turning the referral into an exposed white-label thread.

Keep Private

These details are usually the first things that should not leak out of a referred white-label lane.

Buyer Identity

Brand names, screenshots, internal labels, or identifying artifacts should stay private unless explicitly cleared.

Referrer Role

Keep hidden who introduced the lane, how the handoff happened, and what the referrer should not have to own operationally.

Hidden Economics

Margins, payout splits, markup logic, and private delivery rates should not leak through proof or close-out materials.

Internal Mechanics

Behind-the-scenes workflow, stack, notes, and SOP detail usually should not become part of buyer-facing proof.

Reuse Scope

One approved line or snippet does not automatically approve a testimonial, case study, or broader public story.

Next-Cycle Separation

Keep future work separate so the closed referred step does not quietly become a soft extension.

Reuse Line

Owner Line
