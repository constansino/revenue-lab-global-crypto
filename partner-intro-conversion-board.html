Warm intro conversion page

When the buyer replies to the partner intro, turn that thread into a defined deal fast.

The reply after a warm intro is where momentum either converts or diffuses. This page keeps the conversion step narrow: confirm fit, name the buyer owner, choose one main route, confirm the commercial model, and switch cleanly into payment and handoff.

Conversion Rule

Use the trust of the intro to simplify the next step, not to leave it vague.

Warm intro conversion works when the buyer understands why the fit is real, who owns the next conversation, and what happens after agreement. The goal is definition, not more floating context.

Do Not Do This

Most warm intro conversions fail because the middle of the deal stays fuzzy.

Do Not Keep Three Owners

If nobody knows whether the partner, buyer, or operator owns the thread, the deal will slow down immediately.

Do Not Send More Tabs Than Necessary

A warm buyer does not need a directory. They need one strong route and one clear next action.

Do Not Delay The Payer Question

If the buyer is serious, leaving payment ownership vague only pushes confusion later into the thread.

Do Not Treat Warm Interest Like A Closed Deal

Curiosity is useful, but the deal is not real until route, payer, and movement toward transfer are explicit.

Do Not Split Context Across Chats

When the buyer is warming up, fragmented context usually creates more delay than clarity.
