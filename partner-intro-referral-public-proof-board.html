Referral Public Proof Rule

A referral public proof asset should transfer trust, not the relationship structure that created the win.

The public layer should answer five things fast: what changed, why the proof is believable, what stays hidden, where it belongs first, and what should trigger a wider public asset later.

Proof Shapes

These are the proof shapes that usually travel farthest without overexposure.

Anonymous Result Strip

One line on buyer type, bottleneck, and shift without exposing who the buyer or referrer actually was.

Brandless Before-After Card

A compact summary that shows movement without logos, screenshots, or traceable brand detail.

Quote-Led Proof Block

One approved line with a short context sentence when voice adds trust without needing a longer public story.

Redacted Evidence Tile

One masked artifact when a concrete detail helps more than a plain summary but raw proof would expose too much.

Trust-Focused Note

One public-safe line that supports credibility on a trust page without becoming a broader narrative.

Do-Not-Widen Block

A referral public proof asset that stays valuable precisely because it does not expand into every page and claim at once.
