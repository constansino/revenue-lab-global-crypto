Referral Case Rule

A referral case study should be harder to approve than a single quote, public proof block, or anonymous line.

A bigger proof asset should answer five things first: why a case page is needed at all, what stays invisible, how broad the story can be, where it will be published, and what still needs fresh approval.

Safer Formats

Most referral wins should widen only into one of these narrower case-study forms.

Anonymous Case

Keep the account type, bottleneck, and shift visible while removing the buyer identity entirely.

Brandless Before-After

Explain what changed without logos, screenshots, or page details that expose the private referral context.

Quote-Led Mini Case

Use one approved partner-safe line plus stripped-down business context instead of a larger testimonial package.

Redacted Evidence Case

Use one masked concrete detail only if the proof still needs a visible artifact before a broader audience will trust it.

Public Case Spine

Route into a narrow before-after spine only when the referral path no longer creates extra privacy or relationship risk.

Stay Small Instead

If the bigger asset creates more risk than leverage, keep the win as a small public proof block and stop there.
