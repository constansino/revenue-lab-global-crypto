Client-referral proof unit page

Use one proof-library slot when a referred win should stay reusable without turning into a full public story.

Some closed client-referred wins deserve more than one private line but less than a full case study. That is where a referral proof slot works best. It keeps the asset compact, tagged, reusable, and easier to send later without widening the buyer path, referrer role, or hidden delivery story too early.

Referral Slot Rule

A referral proof-library slot should be smaller than a case story and clearer than a loose note.

The best slot answers five things fast: what the proof unit is, what buyer and referrer details stay hidden, what label it lives under, where it can be reused, and what still needs separate approval before public widening.

Good Slot Types

Most referral proof-library entries should stay inside one of these smaller formats.

Anonymous Result Card

One buyer-hidden line on the account type, bottleneck, first step, and result or clarity shift.

Brandless Before-After Entry

A stripped proof unit with the business problem and visible change, but no traceable brand detail.

Quote-Led Slot

One approved partner-safe sentence plus a light context line, without turning it into a wider testimonial package.

Redacted Evidence Tile

One masked concrete detail when a visible artifact adds trust but a raw screen set would expose too much.

Repeat-Lane Proof Unit

A slot built for similar buyers that helps the partner or client forward the same proof pattern again.

Do-Not-Widen Slot

A small proof unit that stays useful only because it never turns into a long public story.
