Warm intro payer handoff page

Once the warm-intro deal is approved, hand it cleanly from the yes owner to the actual payer.

This page is for the gap after a warm-intro buyer, operator, or internal approver is aligned on the first paid step, but the person who actually moves funds still needs a clean route. The goal is not to restate the whole pitch. The goal is to keep the first step explicit, name the real payer, remove wallet friction, and turn the approval into one transfer path plus a tx hash.

Payer Rule

Warm-intro approval only becomes real when the payment owner receives one narrow route.

Good approval-to-payment handoff keeps the scope fixed and the payer action simple. Confirm who is sending, what exactly the deposit covers, what network and wallet route should be used, and what happens immediately after the hash lands.

Where Warm Deals Leak

Most approval-to-payment stalls in warm intros come from one of these handoff failures.

No Named Payer

Someone on the buyer side said yes, but nobody named the actual person who opens the wallet and sends.

Approval And Payment Are Split

The commercial yes sits with one person while the operational transfer owner sits with another person who never got the full route.

Wallet Friction Is Hidden

The payer may be willing but still uncertain about network, address, exchange steps, or transfer confidence.

Deposit Meaning Is Fuzzy

Payment slows down when the transfer is not tied cleanly to the exact first step and current slot.

Thread Ownership Is Split

Warm intros lose speed when the buyer contact, approver, and payer each think someone else owns the next move.

Short Lines

Use direct lines that move a warm-intro approval into an actual payment event.

Payer Line

Since the first step is already approved, the main thing I want to confirm now is who is actually sending the transfer.

Scope Line

The payment is tied to the exact first step already approved, not to a wider undefined engagement.

Route Line

If wallet routing is the only blocker, I can keep it to one clean path: network, address, transfer, tx hash.

Timing Line

If you still want the current slot, the useful next move is the transfer and hash rather than another internal recap.

Proof Line

Once funds move, send the tx hash in the same thread and I will switch this straight into proof and activation.

Do Not Do This

Approval-to-payment gets slow when the handoff is too broad or too vague.

Do Not Reopen The Approval Case

If the buyer side is already aligned, do not go back to the full internal pitch just because the payer still needs routing.

Do Not Assume The Approver Is The Payer

The person who said yes may not be the person who moves funds. Make that distinction explicit.

Do Not Send Multiple Wallet Options

One clean payment route is easier to execute than several half- explained choices dropped into the thread.

Do Not Let Scope Float

Keep the transfer attached to the narrow approved first step so the payer is not funding a vague future program.

Do Not Separate Payment From Proof

The moment funds move, the next job is tx proof and activation, not another silent delay.
