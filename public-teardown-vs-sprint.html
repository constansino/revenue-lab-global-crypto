Smaller start or full sprint

Choose the first paid move honestly: teardown for proof or sprint for a clear bottleneck.

This page is for the buyer who is not resisting payment itself, but is still choosing between a smaller paid first step and a full sprint deposit. Use teardown when the buyer wants a bounded first commitment, narrower proof, and a lighter payment before bigger scope. Use sprint when the bottleneck is clear enough that delay is already more expensive than the larger deposit.

Decision Rule

Do not use teardown to hide a full sprint or use sprint when the buyer still needs proof.

The right route depends on the shape of the next paid move. If the buyer still wants a smaller paid test, teardown is cleaner. If the next bottleneck already justifies fresh scope, the sprint is cleaner.

Use Teardown First If The Buyer Wants Proof Before A Larger Deposit

Use Sprint When Delay Cost Is Already Bigger Than The Extra Deposit

If the revenue leak is already visible and waiting costs more than the extra deposit gap, moving straight into the sprint is usually more honest than pretending a smaller test is still needed.

If the buyer keeps asking for a larger page, broader flow, or multiple deliverables, do not force that work into the teardown. Break it into a fresh sprint with a fresh deposit.
