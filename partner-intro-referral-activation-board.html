Activation Rule

Referral activation starts when the paid first step becomes an operating thread, not just a commercial yes.

Good referral activation does not explain the whole deal again. It makes five things explicit: what was bought, whether the transfer is pending or proved, who owns the thread now, how visible delivery should be, and what startup packet is sufficient to move now without dragging the referrer back into operations.

Do Not Do This

Most referral activation problems come from leaving the operating handoff half-defined.

Do Not Reopen The Sale

If the first step is already commercially real, activation should not restart persuasion from zero.

Do Not Hide Payment State

Make it explicit whether you still need the transfer, need the proof, or are already clear to start.

Do Not Leave Owner Fuzzy

A paid referred step still stalls when nobody knows who answers operational questions quickly.

Do Not Pull The Referrer Back Into Delivery

The original buyer should not become the default project manager just because they opened the door.

Do Not Separate Proof From Startup

Keep the transfer proof attached to the live start so the commercial side never detaches from execution.
