Referral Case Rule

A referral case study should transfer the lesson of the win, not expose the relationship structure that produced it.

The page should answer five things fast: what changed, why the story matters publicly, what must stay hidden, where it should live first, and what should trigger a wider rollout later.

Case Shapes

These are the referral story shapes that usually travel farthest without overexposure.

Anonymous Mini Case

Show the buyer pattern, the bottleneck, and the shift without revealing who the buyer or original referrer was.

Brandless Before-After

Show the problem and the result without logos, screenshots, or other traceable page detail.

Quote-Led Referral Story

Use one approved voice line when one sentence carries more trust than a heavier public narrative.

Redacted Evidence Story

Use one masked artifact when a concrete detail matters more than a plain summary but raw proof would expose too much.

Public Case With Tight Boundaries

Tell one broader before-after story while keeping the hidden referral path and the private economics off the page.
