Client-referral startup page

After referral payment proof is attached, compress the referred deal into one clean kickoff packet.

This page is for the moment after the deposit is real and the commercial side is effectively closed. The goal now is to stop context drift: confirm who owns the thread, keep the approved first step fixed, send the minimum brief and links, and move into a live start without dragging the original referrer back into startup ops.

Kickoff Rule

Referred deals start fastest when payment proof and the startup packet live in the same operating path.

The first live start should answer five things quickly: who owns the relationship now, what first step was bought, what links matter, what brief is actually needed, and what happens immediately next without routing everything back through the referrer.

Minimum Packet

This is usually enough to start a referred first step cleanly.

Payment Proof

Keep the tx hash attached to the startup packet so commercial confirmation never detaches from execution.

Main Bottleneck

Name the one visible problem the first paid step is supposed to solve before optional ideas start leaking in.

Desired Outcome

State the outcome in practical terms so the kickoff packet is not just a stack of assets with no operating aim.

Relevant Links

Send only the links needed to execute the first step, not a pile of every page the buyer has touched so far.

Decision Owner

Name the person who can answer questions fast enough to keep the kickoff from stalling in silence.

Brief Depth

Decide whether the intake path is enough or whether the brief really needs one deeper pass before work starts.

Short Start Lines

Use direct lines that turn the paid referred deal into a clean start.

Owner Line

Since payment is already confirmed, the main thing I need now is the person who will own questions and approvals during the first step.

Packet Line

The fastest start is the tx hash, the main bottleneck, the desired outcome, and the links that matter for this first step.

Boundary Line

The original referrer does not need to stay in the middle of the startup thread unless they explicitly want visibility.

Scope Line

Kickoff should stay tied to the first paid step already bought, not quietly expand into adjacent work.

Move Line

Once the startup packet is in, the next move is execution, not another round of commercial explanation.

Do Not Do This

Most referred starts slow down because too much gets added after payment.

Do Not Reopen Scope

The paid first step should not become vague again just because the deal has moved into startup.

Do Not Over-Collect

Asking for every possible asset and answer before beginning often creates a fake discovery phase that the buyer did not buy.

Do Not Leave Owner Unclear

Referral kickoffs drift quickly when nobody knows who can make the next small decision.

Do Not Put The Referrer Back Into Startup Ops

The original buyer is not the default startup coordinator just because they made the intro.

Do Not Separate Payment From Start

The proof of payment should remain attached to the startup path so the deal does not split into commercial and delivery threads.
