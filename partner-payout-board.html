Trigger Rules

Keep the payout trigger obvious before work starts.

Client Pays Directly

If the client pays the wallet directly, partner payout should be based on the cleared incoming payment and the agreed partner rule.

Partner Collects First

If the partner invoices or collects from the client on their own side, settlement should be anchored to the agreed internal rate and not re-priced after the fact.

Recurring Work Exception

If repeat work is in play, define yes or no explicitly instead of assuming the first-deal economics will carry forward forever.

Model By Model

Match the payout math to the collaboration type.

Referral Share

Referral deals stay easiest to trust when the partner gets a fixed share on the first cleared sprint instead of open-ended percentage claims on the whole account.

White-Label Margin

White-label partners typically keep the markup they add to a defined delivery rate, so the settlement event is the internal delivery payment rather than a second revenue-share payout.

Reseller Spread

Resellers keep the difference between their client price and the known internal base, which makes the economics easier to repeat.

Retainer Or Next Cycle

If later work is included, state whether that upside is a smaller share, a one-cycle bonus, or no ongoing payout at all.

Settlement Evidence

Close the payout loop with one proof standard.

Confirm The Amount

State the project amount or internal delivery base clearly so the partner can verify what the payout is calculated from.

Confirm The Trigger

Note whether the trigger is cleared client payment, internal transfer received, or the first recurring cycle landing.

Share The Proof

A tx hash, wallet screenshot, or equivalent payment confirmation removes most payout hesitation faster than more explanation.

Archive The Rule

Keep the settlement rule in writing so repeat deals do not reopen the same negotiation every time.
