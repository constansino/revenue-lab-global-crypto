Client-referral payer handoff page

Once a client-referred deal is approved, hand the yes to the actual payer without dragging the referrer back into ops.

This page is for the stage after a client-referred lead or internal approver already said yes to the first paid step, but the person who actually sends funds still needs a clear route. Do not reopen the whole referral story. Keep the approved scope fixed, name the payer, remove wallet friction, and turn the approved deal into one transfer path plus one tx hash.

Payer Handoff Rule

Referral approval only becomes real when one payer receives one narrow transfer route.

Good referral approval-to-payment handoff keeps the scope fixed and the next action simple. Confirm who sends the funds, what the payment covers, what wallet route they should use, and what happens immediately after the hash lands.

Where Referral Deals Leak

Most referred deals slow down here because the approval owner and the payer owner are different people.

Yes Owner Is Not The Payer

Someone approved the first step, but nobody named the person who actually opens the wallet and sends.

The Referrer Gets Dragged Back In

The original buyer should not have to relay finance questions between teams after the new deal is already approved.

Scope Starts Floating Again

Payment slows when the transfer is no longer tied to the narrow approved first step.

Wallet Friction Stays Hidden

The payer may be willing but still uncertain about network, wallet confidence, exchange path, or transfer timing.

No One Owns The Hash Follow-Up

Deals stall after payment because nobody asked for the tx hash in the same thread as the transfer.

Short Lines

Use direct language that turns a referred approval into a real payment event.

Payer Line

Since the first step is already approved, the main thing I want to confirm now is who will actually send the transfer.

Scope Line

The payment is for the exact first step already approved, not for a wider undefined program.

Boundary Line

The original referrer does not need to stay in the middle here unless they specifically want to re-anchor trust.

Wallet Line

If wallet routing is the only blocker, I can keep it to one clean path rather than opening several payment options.

Timing Line

If you still want the current slot, the useful next move is the transfer and hash rather than another internal recap.

Proof Line

Once funds move, send the tx hash in the same thread and I will switch this straight into proof and activation.

Do Not Do This

Referral approval-to-payment gets slow when the handoff becomes broad, fuzzy, or indirect.

Do Not Reopen The Referral Story

Once the internal yes already landed, do not restart the whole case just because the payer still needs routing.

Do Not Treat The Referrer As Finance Ops

The original buyer is not the default wallet explainer, transfer tracker, or payment chase owner.

Do Not Send Several Wallet Paths

One clean transfer route is easier to execute than multiple half- explained choices dropped into the thread.

Do Not Let Scope Drift

Keep the transfer attached to the approved first step so the payer is not funding a vague future program.

Do Not Separate Payment From Proof

The moment funds move, the next job is tx proof and activation, not another silent delay.
