What breaks today

Signing teams lose time when review, approval, and signature state are implicit.

Drafts circulate without a clear owner, redacted copies are made ad hoc, signers get the wrong version, and final delivery is hard to prove when audits, disputes, or internal checks happen later.

What Changes

Turn contract handling into a state machine instead of a chat problem.

One intake gate

Stop collecting draft files, annexes, and signer details from random threads and mail forwards.

One approval sequence

Draft review, internal approval, ready to sign, signed, and delivered should each be visible and explicit.

One final record trail

The team should know which version was approved, which version was sent for signature, and which version became the final archive.

Buyer Signals

If the legal or signing team says these things, the workflow is under-controlled.

"We are not sure which version went to the signer."

That means review and signature readiness are not tied to one visible state model.

"Approvals happen in email and then disappear."

That means the approval trail is fragile and hard to defend later.

"We have the file, but not the proof chain."

That means final delivery exists without a reliable audit-friendly handoff record.
