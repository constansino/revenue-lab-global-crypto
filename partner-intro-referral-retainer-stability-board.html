Referral monthly-rhythm page

After the retainer is chosen, stabilize the monthly rhythm before recurring work gets blurry.

Referral recurring work often feels easy to start because the buyer already trusts the first sprint. That is exactly why the monthly shape needs to be explicit. This page defines the review window, the size of monthly changes, the visibility boundary, and the rule for when a new bottleneck becomes a fresh sprint instead of quietly expanding the retainer.

Stability Rule

A referral retainer stays healthy when monthly work is easy to review and easy to refuse.

Good recurring work is not constant access. It is a recurring operating rhythm the buyer can recognize: what gets reviewed, what gets improved, what stays outside scope, and how to escalate a larger new request into a separate sprint.

What Monthly Stability Needs

These are the pieces that usually make a referral retainer manageable.

Review Cadence

One recurring window to review performance, friction, and the next defined improvements.

Bounded Changes

A clear ceiling on what kinds of tweaks, updates, or small experiments fit inside the monthly package.

Visibility Rule

One explicit rule for whether recurring support stays partner-led, shared, or more direct.

Escalation Trigger

One clear line for when a new issue becomes a separate sprint rather than a retainer tweak.

Proof Trail

The first month should still have its own payment proof and clean monthly commercial boundary.

Monthly Owner

The buyer side should know who receives updates and who can approve changes inside the recurring rhythm.

Short Stability Lines

Use calm language that makes the monthly rule feel structured.

Rhythm Line

The monthly retainer works best with one review window and one defined set of improvements each cycle.

Boundary Line

If a larger new bottleneck shows up, I want to split that into a fresh sprint rather than blur the monthly package.

Visibility Line

Since this came through a referral, I want the recurring visibility boundary to stay explicit as the rhythm settles in.

Proof Line

Even on monthly work, the clean move is one fresh payment proof tied to the first recurring cycle.

Do Not Do This

Most recurring drift starts when nobody can tell the difference between upkeep and new project work.

Do Not Offer Unlimited Access

The retainer should not sound like a standing promise to solve any issue that appears.

Do Not Hide New Sprint Work Inside It

Bigger builds or new bottlenecks should be restated and sold as a separate sprint when they appear.

Do Not Leave The Review Rhythm Vague

If nobody knows when recurring review happens, the monthly work tends to sprawl into reactive noise.

Do Not Drop The Payment Trail

The first monthly cycle still needs its own commercial proof and not just leftover confirmation from the sprint.
