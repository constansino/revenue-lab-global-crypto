When the buyer says yes but the transfer still has not happened, shorten the follow-up path.

This page is for the gap between agreement and payment. The goal is not to restart the sale. The goal is to confirm who is sending the deposit, remove the last blocker, resend the cleanest payment path, and collect the tx hash before momentum leaks out.

Follow-Up Rule

Good follow-up removes friction. It does not reopen the entire close.

If the buyer already agreed, the follow-up should stay narrow. Confirm the payer, confirm the route, confirm the timing, and confirm what happens after the transfer. If a blocker remains, route to the exact page that solves it.

Common Follow-Up Leaks

Most pending payments stall for one of these reasons.

No Named Payer

A deal can be approved but still stall if no one is clearly responsible for sending the transfer.

Wallet Friction

Some payers are ready in principle but pause when they have to choose a network or confirm the address.

Timing Drift

Others simply let the payment slip because the follow-up message never reconnects the transfer to the current build window.

Short Follow-Up Lines

Use calm lines that advance the payment without sounding needy.

Owner Line

Since the scope is already agreed, the only thing I want to confirm is who is sending the deposit so I can route the payment page to the right person.

Friction Line

If the blocker is just the wallet step, I can keep it to one short route: network, address, transfer, tx hash.

Timing Line

If you still want the current slot, the next useful move is the deposit and hash so I can move it into kickoff.
