Referral Approval-Only Rule

Referral approval-only proof works when the note is useful enough to approve but too private to reuse publicly.

The strongest private note answers six things fast: who needs to read it, what referred bottleneck matters, what first paid step or shipped result it refers to, what the original referrer does not need to handle, what stays off-limits publicly, and what happens after the internal yes lands.

What The Note Needs

Most referral approval-only notes work best when they stay inside these six parts.

Approver Audience

Name who the note is for so the language matches the real internal decision instead of sounding broadly promotional.

Referral Bottleneck

State the one operating or revenue leak the referred first step is fixing so the note has commercial clarity.

Defined Step

Tie the note to the exact first paid move or shipped result, not to a vague larger engagement.

Safe Proof Angle

Include one compact result line, shift summary, or quote only if it helps the approver decide now.

Referrer Boundary

Keep the original referrer outside the internal approval loop unless they are explicitly needed for the next step.

After-Yes Route

End with the next concrete move: payment, transfer proof, activation, revision boundary, or handoff.

Good Uses

These are the approval-only note formats that usually move a referred deal fastest.

Founder Sign-Off Note

One short note for the person deciding whether the defined first step is worth buying now.

Finance Approval Note

A tighter note focused on what is being bought, why now, and what payment should unlock after approval.

Operator Readiness Note

A small proof note explaining what will become easier, cleaner, or safer once the first step lands.

Post-Delivery Acceptance Note

A compact note confirming shipped value for acceptance without automatically turning the result into public proof.

Referrer-Hidden Internal Forward

A non-public note the buyer contact can use internally without replaying the original referral path.
