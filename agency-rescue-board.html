Rescue Rule

Do not try to save the whole account at once. Stabilize the commercial leak first.

Rescue work succeeds when it narrows the scope to one urgent bottleneck: offer clarity, reply flow, intake repair, payment path, or client-facing handoff.

Rescue Map

Use a rescue sprint when the account is slipping and time matters.

Overflow Delivery

Use when the agency sold the work but lacks internal bandwidth to tighten the offer, replies, intake, or handoff before the client loses patience.

Stuck Client Account

Use when the client is unconvinced by current progress and needs a visible commercial fix rather than more explanations.

Broken Handoff

Use when leads, approvals, payments, or kickoff steps are being dropped between sales and delivery.

Failed First Pass

Use when the original delivery was too broad, too vague, or too slow, and the partner needs a tighter rescue scope quickly.

Best Rescue Shapes

Pick the smallest sprint that can restore client confidence.

Offer Rescue

Rewrite or reframe the offer so the client sees a cleaner commercial story and a narrower delivery boundary.

Intake Rescue

Tighten forms, qualifying logic, or internal routing so the team stops wasting time and confusing the buyer.

Close-Path Rescue

Repair reply flow, proof order, payment path, or kickoff mechanics so warm interest can move again.

Commercial Logic

Fast rescue work should stay commercially simple.

White-Label First

Default the rescue to a white-label fulfillment model so the partner keeps client trust while the sprint fixes the leak.
