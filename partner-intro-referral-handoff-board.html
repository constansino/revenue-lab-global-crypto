What The Packet Needs

These are the details that usually matter most after a paid client referral.

Owner

Name who can answer questions and who can approve small execution decisions fast enough to prevent delay.

Paid Step

Keep the first step visible inside the handoff so nobody confuses kickoff with a fresh rescoping exercise.

Visibility

State whether the original buyer stays visible, becomes optional, or is fully out for execution clarity.

Relevant Links

Send only the links needed to execute this first step, not every page in the broader referral funnel.

Brief Depth

Decide whether intake is enough or whether the project needs one deeper brief pass before work begins.

Start Path

Make the next move explicit so the handoff packet ends in a start signal, not just more context.

Do Not Do This

Most referral handoffs fail because context gets scattered after payment.

Do Not Reopen The Close

The handoff packet should not restart proof, price, or fit once payment is already attached.

Do Not Leave Owner Vague

A referred project slows down fast when nobody knows who can answer the next operational question.

Do Not Dump Every Link

Too many links create another sorting problem instead of making execution easier.

Do Not Pull The Referrer Back Into Ops

The original buyer should not become the default relay for owner questions, packet gaps, or kickoff timing.

Do Not Hide Visibility Rules

If the original buyer wants to stay in front, or not, that should be explicit before delivery starts.
