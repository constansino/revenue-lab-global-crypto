Client-referral public-page boundary page

Before one closed referral win turns into a public story, freeze the boundary that keeps the lane safe.

A client-referred result can be commercially useful on a public page, but that does not mean the whole private story should go public. This page helps choose the smallest safe public version: a trust proof line, a compact proof card, a quote-led mini story, or a fuller case route only when the buyer, referrer, and hidden economics can stay protected.

Referral Public Story Rule

The best public referral proof is usually smaller than the private relationship path behind it.

A public story should answer five things cleanly: why it needs to be public, what can be shown, what stays hidden, where it will live, and what should still remain inside repeat lanes, slots, or private proof capture.

Public-Safe Formats

Most referral wins should widen into one of these smaller public forms first.

Trust Proof Line

One short public line that makes the process feel credible without unpacking the full private win.

Brandless Public Card

A generic before-after card that shows the shift without naming the buyer or exposing delivery context.

Quote-Led Mini Story

One approved named or role-based line plus a small amount of public-safe context.

Anonymous Public Story

A narrow public page that names the account type and shift while keeping the buyer and referral path hidden.

Proof-Slot Spillover

A compact asset can be public only if it still behaves like one bounded block instead of a broad public claim.

Stay Private Instead

If public use adds more exposure than leverage, keep the proof in repeat lanes, reply threads, or the slot system instead.
