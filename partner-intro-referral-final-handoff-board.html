Final Handoff Rule

Referral handoff works best when the completed step and the next step are clearly separated.

Good final handoff closes the current paid step before anything else starts. It gives the owner what they need to use the work, makes the referrer boundary explicit, and leaves any new scope for a separate decision.

What The Packet Includes

These are the details that usually matter most in the final referral handoff.

Delivered Assets

The final pages, flows, summaries, SOPs, or notes that belong to the completed first step.

Owner Notes

The shortest practical explanation needed so the owner can use the shipped work without guesswork.

Visibility Rules

The final statement on whether future communication stays referrer-visible, shared, or direct.

Scope Boundary

The reminder of what was completed and what belongs in a new cycle if the buyer wants more work.

Proof-Safe Summary

One concise result line that can be reused only if it is approved for partner-safe proof capture.

Next Route

One explicit route after handoff: done, proof capture, renewal, or repeat channel motion.

Short Close-Out Lines

Use direct lines that end the referral cycle without reopening it.

Packet Line

The current step is closed, so I am sending one final packet with the shipped assets, owner notes, and the visibility boundary.

Boundary Line

If there is another bottleneck to fix after this, I want to scope it separately so the completed step stays commercially clean.

Reuse Line

If any of this is useful as proof later, I will route only the approved summary into the partner-safe proof path.

Done Line

With the packet sent and the payment handled, the current cycle is fully closed and the next step can be chosen separately.

Do Not Do This

Most final handoff friction comes from mixing close-out with the next sale.

Do Not Reopen The Paid Step

Once the packet is being handed over, do not let the completed work expand into a fresh revision round or hidden rebuild.

Do Not Dump Every Artifact

The packet should stay useful. Too many links and drafts create another sorting problem instead of a clear close-out.

Do Not Blur Referrer Visibility

If the original buyer stays visible, keep that explicit. Final handoff is a common place for this to drift accidentally.

Do Not Sell The Next Cycle Inside The Packet

Mention the next route if useful, but keep the current handoff separate from any new commercial decision.
