Post-delivery payment page

Confirm the delivery, collect the remaining balance, and close the sprint cleanly.

This page is for the gap after delivery and before close-out. The job is to restate what shipped, confirm whether one light revision is needed inside the agreed boundary, then collect the remaining balance through the same wallet route without reopening the sprint. If another real bottleneck appears right after this work, route it into a fresh renewal instead of stuffing it into the tail end of the finished sprint. If that next move is real but not full-sprint sized yet, use the teardown first. If the next need is recurring upkeep rather than one concentrated sprint, route it into a monthly retainer.

Balance Rule

Tie the remaining balance to delivered value, not pressure.

The cleanest close-out happens after a concise delivery summary and one explicit approval point. Ask for the remaining payment directly, protect the scope boundary, and avoid restarting the sprint while collecting the balance.

Ready-To-Send Lines

Keep the remaining balance ask calm, specific, and bounded.

Standard Balance Ask

The agreed sprint assets are delivered. If everything looks good, the next step is the remaining balance, then I will close the sprint out cleanly.

With Light Revision

If there is one light revision inside the original scope, send it now and I will tighten it before final close-out.

New Scope Reset

If the next request is a broader workflow or second deliverable, I will scope that as a new sprint rather than mixing it into this close-out step.

Renewal Line

If another clear bottleneck appears right after this sprint, I can package it as a fresh sprint with a fresh deposit instead of blurring it into the finished work.

Retainer Line

If the next need is recurring upkeep rather than one bigger new sprint, I can package it as a monthly retainer with its own boundary.

Smaller Next-Step Line

If the next bottleneck is real but still not worth another full sprint yet, I can keep momentum with one paid teardown first.

Short Version

Delivered and ready for approval. If it looks good, send the remaining balance and I will close the sprint cleanly.

Close-Out Boundary

Do not let balance collection reopen the finished sprint.

Light Revision

Small tightening on copy, CTA wording, or structural clarity is a normal close-out step if it stays inside the original sprint.

New Scope

New flows, extra pages, or a second bottleneck belong in a new sprint and should not delay the current balance collection. If the next need is small but recurring, package it as a retainer.

Wallet

Use the same wallet route for the remaining balance.

Consistency removes late-stage doubt. Keep the wallet, preferred network, and payment confirmation request identical from deposit through final balance, then reuse the same route again if the client approves a fresh renewal sprint, a teardown, or the first retainer month.
