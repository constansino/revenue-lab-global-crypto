Activation Rule

Warm-intro activation is when the paid first step becomes an operating thread, not just a commercial yes.

Good activation does not explain the whole deal again. It makes five things explicit: what was bought, whether the transfer is pending or proved, who owns the thread, how visible delivery should be, and what startup packet is sufficient to move now.

Do Not Do This

Most warm-intro activation problems come from leaving the operating handoff half-defined.

Do Not Reopen The Sale

If the first step is already commercially real, activation should not restart persuasion from zero.

Do Not Hide Payment State

Make it explicit whether you still need the transfer, need the proof, or are already clear to start.

Do Not Leave Owner Fuzzy

A paid warm-intro step still stalls when nobody knows who answers operational questions quickly.

Do Not Overbuild Intake

If the minimum packet is enough, do not turn activation into a giant rediscovery process.

Do Not Separate Proof From Startup

Keep the transfer proof attached to the live start so the commercial side never detaches from execution.
