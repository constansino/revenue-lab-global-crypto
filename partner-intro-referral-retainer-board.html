Referral monthly-support page

Turn a closed referral win into a defined monthly retainer without blurring the first sprint.

Sometimes the next best move for the same referred buyer is not another concentrated sprint. It is monthly support around the system that was just installed. This page keeps that transition clean: name the repeating need, package the monthly rhythm, refresh the buyer-side approval, lock the first recurring payment, and keep monthly work separate from larger future rebuilds.

Monthly Rule

Referral retainers work when the first cycle is closed and the monthly job is small, explicit, and repeatable.

The trust from the first step makes recurring work easier to buy, but only if the monthly support has a real shape. Good referral retainers name what gets reviewed each month, what gets improved, what stays outside scope, and how the next payment is locked.

When To Use It

Use this page when the same buyer now needs continuity, not another isolated sprint.

Optimization Need Repeats

Best when the same small tuning, review, or upkeep need will keep showing up around the shipped system.

Leadership Wants Simplicity

Best when one monthly approval is easier than repeated small project decisions for every tiny improvement.

First Sprint Already Proved Trust

Best when the first referral cycle already created enough confidence that recurring support feels credible now.

Still The Same Buyer Context

Best when the monthly work belongs to the same buyer and same system, not when the real opportunity is similar buyers elsewhere.

Not A Hidden Unlimited Scope

Best when the monthly job can stay narrow enough to repeat without becoming a vague all-you-can-ask arrangement.

Fresh Payment Event Needed

A real recurring start should still have its own payment event and proof trail, even if the relationship is already warm.

Short Lines

Use direct language that frames monthly support as rhythm, not loose access.

Rhythm Line

Since the first step is already working, the clean next move may be one defined monthly rhythm for upkeep and optimization.

Boundary Line

The retainer would cover recurring tightening around the current system, not a stream of unrelated new builds.

Approval Line

Even though the relationship is warm, I still want the monthly rule approved cleanly before the first recurring payment lands.

Sprint Split Line

If a larger new bottleneck appears, I will split that into a separate sprint instead of blurring the monthly scope.

Do Not Do This

Most recurring friction comes from treating monthly work like undefined extra help.

Do Not Skip The Monthly Shape

If nobody can describe what happens each month, the retainer is not really packaged yet.

Do Not Hide New Sprint Work Inside It

A major new bottleneck should become a fresh sprint, not a surprise add-on to the recurring package.

Do Not Assume Open-Ended Rights

The first referral win does not automatically imply monthly access, recurring upside, or permanent availability.

Do Not Reuse Old Payment Proof

The first recurring month should have its own payment trigger and tx proof attached to the new monthly cycle.
