Do Not Do This

Most referral deposit-lock attempts fail because they use timing without enough commercial clarity.

Do Not Use It Before Scope Is Clear

If the first step is still fuzzy, tightening the scope matters more than talking about commitment timing.

Do Not Skip Payer Routing

A referred deal can feel approved and still stall if nobody knows who actually sends the transfer.

Do Not Pull The Referrer Back In

The original buyer should not have to manage finance questions once the direct payer is known.

Do Not Reopen The Whole Pitch

Deposit lock should sound like an execution step, not a replay of trust, fit, and proof from the start of the referred thread.

Do Not Force Artificial Urgency

Real urgency comes from delivery bandwidth and timing, not fake countdown language.
