Proof Pack Rule

Warm-intro proof works when approval, proof, and payment stay attached to the same summary.

The best proof pack is short. It shows what the leak was, what shipped, what changed, what is safe to repeat publicly, and what happens next. It should reduce ambiguity, not create another long narrative.

Do Not Do This

Most close-out friction comes from letting the proof pack become vague or overexposed.

Do Not Recount Every Task

The proof pack should summarize the commercial change, not every micro-step that happened during execution.

Do Not Blur Approval And Public Proof

Internal acceptance and reusable public proof are not the same decision, especially in partner-led work.

Do Not Reveal White-Label Mechanics

Keep markup logic, internal delivery mechanics, and partner-side visibility choices out of any proof that was not explicitly approved.

Do Not Reopen Scope In The Summary

The pack should confirm what shipped and what happens next, not turn close-out into a new scoping session.
