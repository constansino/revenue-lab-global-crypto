Case Study Rule

A warm-intro case study should be harder to approve than a single quote or anonymous line.

A bigger proof asset should answer five things first: why a case study is needed at all, what stays invisible, how broad the story can be, where it will be published, and what still needs fresh approval.

Safer Formats

Most warm-intro wins should widen only into one of these narrower case-study forms.

Anonymous Case

Keep the account type, the bottleneck, and the shift visible while removing the buyer identity entirely.

Brandless Before-After

Explain what changed without using logos, screenshots, or page details that expose the private delivery context.

Quote-Led Mini Case

Use one approved partner-safe line plus a stripped-down business context instead of a larger testimonial package.

Redacted Evidence Case

Use one masked concrete detail only if the proof still needs a visible artifact before a broader audience will trust it.

Generic Public Case

Route into a general case-study format only when the intro path no longer creates extra privacy or relationship risk.

Stay Small Instead

If the bigger asset creates more risk than revenue leverage, keep the win as a small proof line and stop there.
