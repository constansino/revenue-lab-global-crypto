Approval-Only Rule

Approval-only proof works when the note is useful enough to approve but too private to reuse publicly.

The best private note answers five things fast: who needs to read it, what bottleneck or shift matters, what first step or shipped result it refers to, what stays off-limits, and what happens after the internal yes lands.

What The Note Needs

Most approval-only notes work best when they stay inside these six parts.

Approver Audience

Name who the note is for so the language matches the real internal decision instead of sounding broadly promotional.

Business Leak

State the one bottleneck or operating risk the first step is fixing so the note has commercial clarity.

Specific Step

Tie the note to the defined first paid move or shipped result, not to a vague larger engagement.

Safe Proof Angle

Include one compact result line, shift summary, or quote only if it helps the approver decide now.

Off-Limits Boundary

Keep names, screenshots, margins, and public reuse rights outside the note unless they are explicitly cleared.

After-Yes Path

End with the next concrete move: payment, deposit, activation, revision boundary, or handoff.

Good Uses

These are the approval-only note formats that usually move a warm-intro lane fastest.

Founder Sign-Off Note

One short note for the person deciding whether the first step is worth buying now.

Finance Approval Note

A tighter note focused on what is being bought, why now, and what payment should unlock.

Operator Readiness Note

A small proof note explaining what will become easier, cleaner, or safer once the step lands.

Partner Internal Forward

A non-public note a partner can use inside their own lane without turning it into a forwardable proof asset.

Post-Delivery Approval Note

A small note confirming shipped value for acceptance without automatically opening public proof reuse.

Revision Boundary Note

A note clarifying what is approved now and what belongs in a separate next step rather than inside this cycle.
