Warm intro case-study page placement

Place one warm-intro win on a public case-study page without publishing the whole private path behind it.

A public case-study page can help a closed warm-intro win travel farther, but it still needs a narrow spine. The best version is usually one before-after story, one clear bottleneck, one approved shift, and one page destination. That gives public leverage without exposing the hidden intro path, raw economics, or the full delivery context.

Public Case Rule

A warm-intro case-study page should stay like one narrow before-after story, not a public download of every hidden detail.

The page should answer five things fast: what changed, why the story is useful publicly, what remains hidden, which format is safe, and when the page should stay smaller than a generic case-study rollout.

Case-Safe Formats

Most warm-intro public case-study pages should start with one of these narrower story shapes.

Anonymous Mini Case

Show the account type, the bottleneck, and the shift without identifying the buyer.

Brandless Before-After

Show the problem and the outcome without logos, screenshots, or traceable page detail.

Quote-Led Mini Story

Use one approved sentence and one context line when a voice cue increases trust without needing a larger testimonial package.

Redacted Evidence Story

Use one masked artifact when a concrete detail matters more than a plain summary, but raw proof would expose too much.

Public Case With Tight Boundaries

Tell one broader before-after story while keeping the hidden intro path and the private economics off the page.
