Warm intro proof reuse page

After the final handoff is done, capture one proof asset without exposing the warm-intro path.

This page is for the stage after the warm-intro step is paid, handed over, and commercially closed. The next job is not more delivery. It is proof capture with discipline: choose one approved result line, preserve the partner boundary, keep the buyer anonymous if needed, and route the proof into repeat intros or a separate next cycle without reopening the finished work.

Proof Capture Rule

The best warm-intro proof is usually one small approved line, not a full public case study.

Warm-intro deals often depend on privacy and trust. Good proof capture respects that. Start from the closed packet, ask for the smallest usable summary, freeze what stays private, and only then route the proof into future intros or repeat channel motion.

What Is Usually Safe

Most warm-intro proof should stay inside these tighter boundaries.

Anonymous Outcome Line

The type of account, the bottleneck, the shipped first step, and one result or clarity shift are usually the safest reusable layer.

Partner Quote

One short line from the partner about what became easier can work well if it does not expose the buyer or the white-label setup.

Brandless Summary

A stripped-down before-after summary often travels better than a detailed case study when the intro relationship is sensitive.

Explicit Off-Limits List

Reuse Destination

The approved proof should point somewhere practical: a new intro, repeat lane, or one narrow renewal conversation.

Commercial Separation

Do Not Do This

Most proof-capture mistakes come from treating a warm intro like a fully public case.

Do Not Assume Naming Rights

Even if the result is strong, do not assume the buyer or partner name can be used publicly.

Do Not Reopen Scope

Proof capture should not become another review cycle or an excuse to renegotiate what the completed step was worth.

Do Not Expose The Intro Mechanics

Keep the hidden relationship map, payout logic, and internal operating details out of the reusable proof layer.

Do Not Overbuild The Asset

A short approved line is usually more useful than a long page that takes weeks to approve.
