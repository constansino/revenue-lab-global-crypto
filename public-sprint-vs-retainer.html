Route decision page

Choose the next paid lane honestly: fresh sprint or bounded retainer.

This page is for the buyer who agrees something should happen but is still unclear about the shape of the next paid move. Use a sprint when the next bottleneck is new, concentrated, and needs a defined push. Use a retainer when the work is recurring, bounded, and mainly about upkeep, iteration, or monthly optimization.

Decision Rule

Do not hide a new sprint inside a retainer or stretch a retainer into a new sprint.

Buyers trust the route more when the package shape matches the work shape. New concentrated work should be sold as a sprint. Recurring bounded work should be sold as a retainer.

Route Checks

Use these checks to pick the correct lane.

Use A Sprint When The Bottleneck Is New

If the next problem is a fresh sales, automation, or conversion bottleneck that needs one concentrated delivery push, sell a sprint, not a retainer.

Open Public Pricing Open Public Renewal

Use A Retainer When The Asset Is Already Live

If the work is monthly optimization, bounded support, monitoring, or upkeep on something already shipped, a retainer is the cleaner route.

Open Public Retainer

Do Not Use Retainer To Hide New Scope

If the buyer keeps adding a new larger bottleneck, break it out into a new sprint instead of quietly stretching the recurring lane.

Open Public Scope Boundary Open Public Renewal

Do Not Use Sprint For Small Recurring Tasks

If the work is mostly recurring and bounded, a new sprint can feel heavier than the need. Use the retainer lane instead.

Open Public Retainer

Use Teardown If The Buyer Still Needs A Smaller First Paid Move

If the buyer agrees on the direction but still wants a tiny first step before a bigger sprint or retainer, use teardown to keep momentum.

Open Public Teardown

Keep Payment Explicit Either Way

Once the lane is chosen, the next move should be payment through the same visible wallet, then tx hash back in the thread.
