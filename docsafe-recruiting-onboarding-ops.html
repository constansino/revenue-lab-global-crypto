What breaks today

Recruiting teams lose time when candidate paperwork is scattered and status is ambiguous.

CVs, IDs, work eligibility files, offer documents, and client approvals arrive in different channels. Operators spend time chasing missing items and rebuilding the candidate trail by hand.

What Changes

Turn candidate paperwork into an operational flow instead of a reminder loop.

One intake gate

Candidates and internal teams know exactly which files are needed and which formats are accepted.

One approval sequence

Waiting on candidate, waiting on client, compliance review, onboarding-ready, and delivered should be explicit.

One handoff record

The team should be able to prove which files were received, which files were approved, and what final package was handed over.

Buyer Signals

If recruiting ops says these things, the workflow gap is already costing time and placements.

"We keep chasing missing candidate documents."

That means intake is not controlled and operators are spending too much time on follow-up instead of progression.

"We are not sure if the candidate is actually onboarding-ready."

That means compliance, client approval, and file completeness are not tied to one visible state model.

"The handoff to client ops gets messy."

That means the team lacks a final package and proof layer for the placement or onboarding file set.
