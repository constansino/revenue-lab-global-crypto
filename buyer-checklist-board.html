Neutral decision page

Turn "I need to think" into one clean next decision.

Most buyers do not need more persuasion. They need one clearer decision frame. This page helps them check whether the real blocker is scope, risk, internal approval, commitment size, or timing so the next step can stay small and explicit.

Checklist Rule

Thinking usually means one unresolved question, not total resistance.

The job is to identify that one question fast. If the buyer can name the bottleneck, accept the boundary, and approve the next step, the decision gets much easier. If not, route them to the right smaller page instead of pushing harder.

Buyer Checklist

Use these five checks before asking for a deposit.

1. Is The Bottleneck Clear?

If the buyer still cannot name the main commercial problem, they are not really deciding yet. They are still diagnosing.

Open Pricing Board

2. Is The Scope Narrow Enough?

If the package still feels broad or fuzzy, the decision will feel bigger than it should. Narrow the scope before asking for speed.

Open Risk Control

3. Do They Need Internal Sign-Off?

If someone else needs to approve the spend or the priority, the real blocker is internal alignment, not buyer confidence alone.

Open Approval Pack

4. Do They Need A Smaller First Step?

If the buyer likes the direction but wants lower initial commitment, reduce the step size instead of arguing abstractly.

Open Micro Offer

5. Are They Ready To Lock The Window?

If the bottleneck is clear, the scope is controlled, and the next move is approved, stop reopening the whole deal and move to deposit.

Open Deposit Lock

If The Answer Is No

Route the buyer to the page that resolves the exact blocker.

Still Comparing Scope

If the buyer is stuck between diagnosis, sprint, or broader setup, pricing logic is the first page, not payment.

Still Managing Risk

If the buyer wants the deal to feel safer, reduce ambiguity with clearer boundaries and staged commitment.

Still Protecting Cash

If the buyer wants a smaller first yes, a micro offer or audit is cleaner than forcing the main sprint early.

Short Decision Lines

Use calm language that reduces analysis paralysis.

Checklist Line

The fastest way to think about it is to check whether the blocker is scope, risk, approval, or commitment size. Once that is clear, the next step is usually obvious.

Scope Line

If the scope still feels broader than one bottleneck, let us narrow it first instead of forcing a bigger yes.

Commitment Line

If the fit is already clear, the next move is the deposit step, not another wide discussion loop.

Clean decisions close faster than overloaded decisions.
