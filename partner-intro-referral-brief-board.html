Brief Items

These are the deeper details that most often matter after referral intake.

Paid First Step

Keep the approved first step visible so the brief does not become an excuse to reopen scope.

Current Leak

Describe the specific operational leak the first step should reduce first.

Desired Outcome

State the measurable shift the buyer wants from this step rather than a vague long-term aspiration.

Relevant Links

Pull only the pages, docs, screenshots, recordings, or repos that explain this first step.

Constraints

Note any non-negotiable brand, timing, tooling, or compliance constraints before work begins.

Stakeholders

Name who needs visibility, who can answer quickly, and who gives final approval if revisions are needed.

Referrer Boundary

Confirm whether the original buyer stays visible, becomes optional, or steps fully out for operating speed.

Do Not Do This

Most referral brief drag comes from turning one deeper pass into a second discovery cycle.

Do Not Reopen Scope

The paid first step should stay fixed while the brief adds delivery clarity.

Do Not Rebuild Intake

If the key inputs already exist, do not ask for the same packet twice.

Do Not Blur Owner

A deeper brief still fails when nobody knows who can answer the next operational question quickly.

Do Not Pull The Referrer Back In By Default

The original buyer should not become the fallback project manager just because the brief got deeper.

Do Not Delay Kickoff After Clarity

Once the deeper details are captured, move into kickoff rather than building another admin loop.
